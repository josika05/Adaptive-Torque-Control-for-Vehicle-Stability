PROJECT: Adaptive Vehicle Torque Control System

--------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 1: DRY CONDITION (NO YAW)
--------------------------------------------------
Inputs:
Torque = 100 Nm
Friction = 0.9 (Dry)
Yaw Rate = 0
Wheel Speed ≈ Vehicle Speed

Observation:
- Slip ratio is very low
- No yaw (vehicle is stable)
- High friction (good road grip)

Result:
Final Torque = 100 Nm

Conclusion:
No torque reduction is applied because the vehicle is in a stable condition.

--------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 2: DRY + YAW CONDITION
--------------------------------------------------
Inputs:
Torque = 100 Nm
Friction = 0.9 (Dry)
Yaw Rate = 0.5 (> threshold)
Wheel Speed ≈ Vehicle Speed

Observation:
- Slip is low
- Yaw rate is high (vehicle turning/unstable)

Result:
Final Torque ≈ 90 Nm

Conclusion:
Torque is reduced to maintain stability during turning even though the road is dry.

--------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 3: WET CONDITION
--------------------------------------------------
Inputs:
Torque = 100 Nm
Friction = 0.4 (Wet)
Yaw Rate = 0
Wheel Speed slightly higher than Vehicle Speed

Observation:
- Low friction (poor grip)
- Moderate slip possible

Result:
Final Torque ≈ 80–90 Nm

Conclusion:
Torque is reduced to prevent wheel slip on wet roads.

--------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 4: SLIP CONDITION
--------------------------------------------------
Inputs:
Torque = 100 Nm
Friction = 0.8
Yaw Rate = 0.1
Wheel Speed >> Vehicle Speed

Observation:
- Slip ratio is high (> threshold)
- Risk of wheel spinning

Result:
Final Torque ≈ 75–85 Nm

Conclusion:
Torque is reduced to control excessive slip.

--------------------------------------------------
SCENARIO 5: WET + YAW (CRITICAL CONDITION)
--------------------------------------------------
Inputs:
Torque = 100 Nm
Friction = 0.3 (Wet)
Yaw Rate = 0.6 (High)
Wheel Speed >> Vehicle Speed

Observation:
- Low friction
- High yaw instability
- High slip

Result:
Final Torque ≈ 60–70 Nm

Conclusion:
Maximum torque reduction is applied to ensure safety in highly unstable conditions.

--------------------------------------------------
OVERALL SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------
- Stable conditions → No torque reduction
- Yaw detected → Moderate reduction
- Wet/slip conditions → Higher reduction
- Combined conditions → Maximum reduction

The system adapts torque dynamically to maintain vehicle stability.
